# obvious_branding.md

High-probability "obvious" responses for naming, positioning, and brand
creative briefs. Used to anchor originality scoring in branding runs.

---

- a short memorable name ideally two syllables
- use an invented word or a portmanteau
- use an animal metaphor suggesting trust or speed
- reference a natural element like mountain river sky
- use latin or greek roots for authority
- name it after the founder
- name it after the core verb of the action
- name it with a single evocative noun
- use alliteration for recall
- make it sound like a trusted incumbent but younger
- use a colour as the name
- use a number as the name
- make the logo a simple geometric form
- use a wordmark with custom lettering
- the brand should feel premium through serif typography
- the brand should feel approachable through sans serif and soft colour
- position against the category leader
- position as the opposite of the incumbent
- position as the affordable alternative
- position as the premium alternative
- position for a niche audience first then expand
- the brand voice is warm and human
- the brand voice is direct and unfussy
- the tagline is an instruction
- the tagline is a promise
