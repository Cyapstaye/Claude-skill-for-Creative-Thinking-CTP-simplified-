# Clarify — taxonomy reference

> **Dispatch-forced.** Reached only via the clarify mech above. Provides the four-axis taxonomy and the when-not-to-ask rule.

## Axis 1 — Purpose

The query does not make clear *what the output is for*. Is the user
exploring a concept, making a decision, drafting a thing to ship, or
probing their own intuition?

An output for thinking-with can be rougher, more branching, less
committed. An output to ship must collapse to one artifact. An output to
persuade someone else has a specific reader and a specific claim; the
register changes.

## Axis 2 — Audience

The query is clear on purpose but ambiguous on *who receives the output*.
An output for oneself differs in register, density, and scaffolding from
one for a boss or a public reader. Tone, jargon density, and the amount of
framing all track audience.

## Axis 3 — Constraint

The query is clear on purpose and audience but leaves *what the output
must respect* ambiguous — existing commitments, a format, a length, a
material, a regulatory frame, etc. A reframe that ignores a live
constraint is not a reframe; it is a daydream.

## Axis 4 — Scope

The query is clear on purpose, audience, and constraints but leaves *how
wide the field is* ambiguous — one angle or a survey; a single artefact
or a family; a narrow answer or an exploratory response.

## When not to ask

Do not ask when:

- The query is under-specified but has an obvious default reading.
- The query is vague in a way that the divergent Stage-1 profiles are
  literally designed to explore. Stage 1 thrives on vagueness; it is a
  feature of the protocol.
- The ambiguity is about tone, length, or format. Those are handled at
  emit.

## Multi-axis-ambiguity failure mode

If two axes are both ambiguous enough to produce materially different
outputs, the clarify step must fail pre-flight, not cascade into two
questions. The cost of asking twice is larger than the cost of asking the
user to resubmit. Record `"ambiguity_on_two_axes"` and stop. The user
resubmits with enough context that one axis becomes dominant; the new
run's clarify step asks (or does not ask) on that single axis.
